Grading and Expectations

Students are expected to complete the assigned readings before coming to class. Quizzes will announced throughout the semester to ensure students keep up with the readings. Readings preceded by “Additional resource” are not required, but are offered as pointers in case they are helpful.

Graded work will take the following forms:

  • Homework
    Assignments will be distributed through Canvas. Programming assignments should be submitted as .ipynb files through Canvas, while written assignments can either be submitted through Canvas or handed in physically during class.
    Collaboration policy: Except when specified otherwise, you may work in groups of up to three total students on homework. Each student must turn in their own work, and you must list the names of all group members after your name. You are encouraged to use Canvas to ask questions about the homework and share your findings, as long as you do not post so much as to give away the solution to others.
    Late policy: Late assignments will receive 80% of credit up to one day late, and 50% of credit if two days late. Assignments will not be accepted after two days.
  • Exams
    There will be one midterm exam, approximately two thirds through the semester, and a final exam during the official exam time and location (see schedule).
  • Attendance and Quizzes
    Attendance is required and will be checked on random days. If you are unable to attend a class, you should notify the instructor before the class to avoid a grade penalty. If an in-class quiz (which will be announced) is missed without an excused absence, the quiz grade will be zero (make-up quizzes are not offered, but they can be excused for a valid absence).

Final grade percentages will be weighted according to the distribution to the right. Letter grades will follow a typical scoring distribution (A if >= 93%, A- if >= 90%, B+ if >= 87%, B if >= 83%, B- if >= 80%, C+ if >= 77%, and so on). Do not expect grades to be curved, though exam grades may be curved if needed.

We will give you feedback on assignments, quizzes, and exams within 7 days. Grades will be provided through Canvas.
